## Saturday - 27th June 2026

Hello again! It's been a while.

A little more than a year ago I stopped maintaining my blog to gleefully slide into weed-induced psychosis. Reading back through the [old posts](https://stuffedwomb.at/blogagain), I can now see how I wrestle for control, eventually give up and  pivot to obsessing about my creative process, desperately avoiding the plastic-bottle-bong in the room.

Getting out of that psychosis was a painful process. I feel like I lost months to paralysis, to all the emotions that the drug had been supressing and to coming to grips with how far from myself I'd gotten. 

Somehow I managed to meaningfully contribute to the [Mosa Lina 2nd Layer Update](https://store.steampowered.com/news/app/2477090/view/502825815722952069) and [FALLSTRUKTUR](https://store.steampowered.com/app/3674270/FALLSTRUKTUR/) before relapsing. 

Now, almost one year and a couple of relapses later, I've been clean & sober for longer than I've ever been since turning 15. I'm very proud of that and feel pretty good.

Work on NEXT GAME has been ramping up steadily for the last few months. We should be able to announce it soon. The Mosa Lina money is defenitely running out now & there's flashes of anxiety about money again.

If NEXT GAME fails financially it would be a pity, but I reckon I'd have enough ressources left to do one last commercially viable project before being completely broke.

Besides that, I've also been working hard on trusting the process, on looking into the future without fear. Something will work out. It always has in the past. Survivorship-bias-mentality, basically :)

But yes. Blogposts.

Last time I wrote these things I had a clear agenda, I wanted to write myself into productivity again. That did not work, so now I guess I'll just push myself a little bit to write about whatever comes to mind, to see if one of the big essays I've been wanting to tackle for years now rears its head.

Let's see what happens :)



## Sunday - 5th July 2026

When the intense heatwave that had been burning braincells across Vienna finally ended, my body took that as a signal collapse inward. Climate change driven exhaustion, aided by intense overeating and too many dates, left me hollow and depleted in a deep way.

Before this period of hull-ness, most days contained extremely intense emotions rising out of nothing, taking over my whole being. One time there was deep joy and fulfilment, but mostly it was all rage and sadness and inadequacy.

My 20s were all about this bouncing between elation and depression, flirting with various mood disorder diagnosis and then escaping all of this by immersing myself in the act of making games, leading to a somewhat prolific output that neatly tracks the strenght of my desire to not be alone with myself, a product of trying to gain distance from inner turmoil by redirecting these bursts of energy and these waves of fear into a craft and a practice.

I've been griefing these intense emotions, associating them with a highly productive time, but now that they are back I am reminded why I wanted to stabilise my whole life. 

It is not easy to find access to the modes of creative work I slipped in and out of as a younger person. I have been trying to make peace with the fact that things might never be the same, that the intense feeling of belonging and cohesion that came with extended period of intentional work (especially on PRODUCER and Mosa Lina), might never come back, that the price to pay for it is, literally, too high.

And so I have been trying to stick with the work on the next game, to be patient with it, to accept that things are just not clicking into place right now and that, at some point, everything will come together, as it usually does. It did not work this week, perhaps because I was anticipating the moment of coherence too much, because I was expecting it as a reward for slog-like suffering through a bunch of designs that never lived up to what I was expecting of them.

I have been running around Europe these last few years, telling every student I was allowed to talk in front of that if they don't enjoy the work, there is no point in doing it. The rewards are too uncertain and meagre to justify a process defined by pain only. I should listen to that advice myself and actively try to find the joy in the process, rather than expecting it to be delivered to me as a reward for suffering enough.

Just a few hours ago I snapped out of the depression, watching the sunset while riding my bike along the canal. 

I feel like I should read a bunch of contemporary design thoughts before conributing my own and I am really excited by [Warp Point](https://www.warppoint.games/?ref=readonlymemo.com) and how just a little bit of following links has led me to some very interesting stuff. Perhaps this webring is what finally pushes me over the edge of learning what RSS actually is.

That would make at least one person happy, I think ;)

See you next weekend. Take care.

Josh
